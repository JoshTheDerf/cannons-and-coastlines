How this came about

Originally I was just trying to make a silly little game to play with my friends and the youth at my church — something like Battleship that involved shooting real cannons, alternating between chaos and strategy.

I threw together a few rough models in TinkerCad just to see if the idea would work, took them to my office to play around with, and it kind of blew up from there. Now I really want to play this game. My friends want to play it too. And apparently a ton of other people on the internet would as well.

So we're trying to figure out: are there enough people interested to make this worth investing the time and money to turn it into a real game, or should it stay as a fun prototype?

To that end, my wife and I committed to print, package, and ship ten starter packs — and all ten are now claimed. We're capped at ten at a time because each pack is printed, packed, and shipped by hand out of our home, and that takes a real chunk of time. Once these are out the door, we'll see whether we can run another batch.

If you'd still like one, the form below now feeds a waiting list for future runs. We'll reach out when we have a clearer sense of timing and what the next batch will cost — pricing may shift from this first run.

Pack contents

For a full game, you'll probably want two packs. But you can play a stripped-down game with one.

A full ship set

  • Full fleet: your choice of the Corsair Fleet (4 small ships) or Queen's Fleet (3 larger ships)
  • Masts, sails, and flag holders for all ships
  • A pack of cannons, cannonballs, and coins
  • Movement wheels and cargo for all ships
  • Three island toppers and flagpoles
  • Rocks and reefs

Printable manual

  • The current edition of the rulebook
  • Printable faction card
  • (And we'll make sure you always get the latest version to print off.)

As I keep working on the models and adding new pieces, I'll do my best to keep everything backwards-compatible with this run.
